London - France - San Francisco

May 2nd, 2016

I finished exams on the 14th of April, two weeks earlier than normal because the business school works on their own schedule. On the 18th, I flew to Paris to meet up with my parents and my Uncle Brad. I cut it extremely close with my Chinese visa, picking it up around 1pm on the day when my flight was at leaving at 7, but it luckily worked out.



We arrived in Paris early morning after taking a red eye flight, so we started the trip with a quick bite to eat followed by a nap. In the afternoon we walked around close to our hotel, passing by the Louvre, wandering over to the island, and the Notre Dame Cathedral. At dinner I ordered a shrimp and avocado salad to start, and couldn’t believe it came with a full avocado. I don’t think I’ve ever gotten that back in Canada. This still blows my mind.


The second day we visited the Musée D’Orsay and saw lots of beautiful art. The day after we went out to Versailles and it was pretty hard to believe it was actually someone’s place of residence. From the hall of mirrors to the courtyard, it seemed never ending.


That night, we went to see the Eiffel Tower just as the sun was setting.


One of my favourites parts of the trip was the Catacombes. Thousands of people were buried underground Paris, and you can walk through a 2km stretch of it. Really, really cool.


To finish off our stint in Paris before heading up North, we checked out the Arc de Triomphe and the Avenue des Champs-Élysées.


On the same day we arrived in Arras we went to the Canadian memorial at Vimy ridge. The shame with pictures is that they really do not show the scale of these things. Only having seen pictures of the monument (mainly on the $10 bill), actually arriving there was something else. It baffled me how there were cemeteries everywhere you would look.


This next picture was probably the most interesting thing of the trip. Every single site we visited had other people at it, even if it was a cemetery of only a couple hundred. Pictured below is the German cemetery, with 4 people buried for each cross. 44,000 people were buried in this one site, and no one visited. The parking lot was so small we actually drove past it the first time.


A French cemetery is below, along with a great memorial. It has the list of every single person that fought in the war listed without country, race, religion, anything. Just all of the names blended together.


The next day we took a trip to Beaumont Hamel, the Newfoundland memorial site. On July 1st 1916, 780 Newfoundlanders “attacked” the Germans at this site. 68 answered role call the next day. Seeing it you really understood how this was such a slaughter… They never stood a chance. The danger tree (pictured left) was a landmark where just about no one got past. It really was not far from where they came out of the trenches. The winding trenches were all around, and the Newfoundlanders would have had to jump out of the only to know to be shot down almost immediately.


This Caribou memorial is one of five in France & Belgium all commemorating the casualties faced by the Newfoundland Regiment in the first World War, with another back in St. John’s.


Even the tiny town of Arras had lots of history, and served as a home base for people to go to in order to visit all the memorial sites nearby. This below is a picture of the city center at night.


The next day we took a quick road trip to Belgium to go to the Flanders field museum where there was actually an exhibition on Canada on display, so that was great. You could get to the roof of the church for a great view, but inside there was so much. It was really well done. Below is a hat from the Newfoundland Regiment, and a picture of the church right behind the museum.


After having a bit of a scare about not being able to get back to Paris (there was a train strike and our initial train got cancelled), we eventually got back without too much of a delay and went on a gorgeous dinner boat tour.


That was it for France! The next morning (April 27th) I got up early for a flight to San Francisco via Toronto for my first Cansbridge Fellowship Conference. With one day of free time, we rented bikes and went all around, eventually over the bridge to Sausalito. A bit jet lagged, we had an early night to get ready for the start of the weekend. I didn’t take many pictures, but the second picture of the bridge was taken from Google’s office! Definitely loved SF, and can’t wait to get back.
