Newfoundland, Summer 2015

August 20th, 2015

It’s always good getting back to Newfoundland, and I don’t think that’ll ever change. Now that I don’t live there I always try and get outside a lot while I’m home. I was pretty lucky with the weather, as can be seen in all the pictures. I started my time at home taking Riv (my parent’s dog) for a walk around signal hill. Believe it or not, the first two pictures are of where my sister got married in the summer of 2014. The weather wasn’t even close to the pictures here though… Sheet white fog with gale force winds made for a memorable outdoor wedding.


I also love how when I’m home I can pretty much always find a game of volleyball. Played in my first grass tournament, so that was interesting.


A couple days later I went for an overnight hike with two friends (Andrew and Aanch) at the Spout. It wasn’t a particularly difficult hike, roughly 13km each way, but the sights were breathtaking. We had our lunch in the top of this lighthouse, which seemed to be abandoned (smashed window).


When we got to the spout, it was cooler than we could’ve imagined. We sat there and watched rainbows literally form, over and over again. As the waves would come in it would shoot water up (hence it being called the Spout), but because of the time there was a clear rainbow created every time.


Unfortunately, cameras don’t work well in the night, but we had a fire then watched a meteor shower on probably the nicest day of the summer. I really couldn’t get over how lucky we were with the weather. There was literally frost less than two weeks before we did this hike. We got up early to see the sunrise. One perk of being at some of the most easterly points in North America means that nothing will be in the way of the sunrise.


For my last weekend home, we took a family trip out to the cabin and out on the boat. This is what I miss about Newfoundland. Well, obviously there’s other things, but living in London, Ontario, it’s basically impossible to get this kind of thing. See ya Christmas NL.
