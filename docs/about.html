About Me

Who is Mike Allan?

Mike Allan is an ambitious and driven student enrolled at Western University, originally from the fantastic province of Newfoundland. He is pursuing a degree in Mechatronic Systems Engineering while concurrently completing an Honors in Business Administration from the Ivey Business School as a Loran Scholar.

He describes himself by the George Roter coined phrase — a "pragmatic idealist" because he does not think the polarity of realism vs. idealism should be a dichotomy. His work with Engineers Without Borders Canada has played a significant role in his personal development, both at his university's chapter and throughout his time working at the National Office in the summer of 2014. He is Western University's Chapter President for the 2016-17 school year.

He has a wide range of passions, including athletics, music, languages, entrepreneurship, technology, and social change. Mike is striving to find a way to amalgamate his passions for business, technology, and social justice. To contact him, please head over to the contact page.

What is Mike currently doing?

Right now, Mike is interning with Husky Energy in Shenzhen, China as part of the Cansbridge Fellowship Program. He just finished his first year of the HBA1 program at the Ivey Business School and will be going back to engineering next year to complete his fourth year of study at Western. Mike is hoping to do lots of travel this summer, keep a blog, and take on a few side projects.
